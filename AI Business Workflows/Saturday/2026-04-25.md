# Saturday — HR/Recruiting AI Business Workflows

**Date pulled:** 2026-04-25 (Asia/Dhaka)  
**Window targeted:** last 30 days  
**Theme:** How businesses are using AI to replace/automate HR and recruiting functions

## Executive summary
The integration of agentic AI into HR and recruiting workflows is transforming traditional processes by automating end-to-end tasks. This shift allows organizations to streamline operations, reduce time-to-hire, and enhance candidate engagement while maintaining compliance and fairness. However, businesses must approach implementation cautiously, ensuring human oversight in critical decision-making areas.

## Top 5 practical use cases
1. **Automated Candidate Sourcing**: Agentic AI can autonomously search multiple job boards and databases, refining criteria based on previous hiring successes. This reduces the time spent on sourcing candidates significantly.
   
2. **Screening and Shortlisting**: AI agents evaluate candidates holistically, identifying non-obvious matches and generating evidence-based shortlists, which can cut screening time by up to 75% when governed properly.

3. **Interview Scheduling**: AI tools can manage complex scheduling across time zones, coordinating interviews without manual intervention, thus minimizing delays and candidate drop-off rates.

4. **Feedback and Communication**: Automated systems can handle candidate outreach, providing personalized communication and follow-ups based on candidate responses, enhancing engagement.

5. **Onboarding Automation**: AI can orchestrate onboarding processes, ensuring new hires receive necessary training and resources while tracking compliance and progress.

## Best first use case to implement
**Automated Candidate Screening**: This is a practical first pilot for businesses looking to leverage agentic AI. By implementing an AI-driven screening workflow, organizations can reduce the time spent on manual evaluations while ensuring a consistent rationale for candidate selection. This use case allows for a clear measurement of efficiency gains and candidate quality, making it easier to justify further investments in AI.

## OpenClaw workflow mapping
1. **Input**: Job requisition details and candidate criteria.
2. **Sourcing**: AI agent autonomously searches multiple platforms for candidates.
3. **Screening**: The agent evaluates candidates against predefined criteria, generating a shortlist.
4. **Outreach**: Automated personalized emails are sent to shortlisted candidates.
5. **Scheduling**: The agent coordinates interview times with candidates and interviewers.
6. **Feedback Loop**: Collects data on candidate responses and hiring manager feedback to refine future sourcing and screening criteria.

## 7-day KPI pilot
- **Day 1**: Set up the AI screening tool and define candidate criteria.
- **Days 2-3**: Run the sourcing process and generate a shortlist of candidates.
- **Days 4-5**: Conduct interviews and gather feedback from hiring managers.
- **Days 6-7**: Analyze time-to-hire, candidate quality, and engagement metrics.

**KPIs to measure**:
- Time taken from job requisition to candidate shortlist.
- Quality of shortlisted candidates (measured by interview performance).
- Candidate engagement rates (response rates to outreach).
