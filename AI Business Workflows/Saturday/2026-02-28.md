# Saturday — HR/Recruiting AI Business Workflows
**Date pulled:** 2026-02-28 (UTC)  
**Window targeted:** last 30 days (2026-01-29 → 2026-02-28)  
**Theme:** How businesses are using AI to replace/automate HR and recruiting functions

## Executive Summary
Last-30-day signal suggests the clearest near-term automation pattern is **recruiting operations compression**: candidate sourcing support, initial screening, interview coordination, job ad distribution, and ATS orchestration are increasingly handled by AI-enabled workflows. Human recruiters remain most valuable in final evaluation, stakeholder alignment, and candidate experience management.

## Top 5 Practical Use Cases (HR/Recruiting)
1. **Candidate screening and shortlist prep automation**
   - AI ranks incoming applicants, flags qualification gaps, and generates recruiter-ready summaries.
2. **Interview scheduling + follow-up orchestration**
   - AI handles high-volume logistics (availability matching, reminders, reschedules, nudges).
3. **Programmatic job advertising optimization**
   - AI adjusts posting spend/allocation across channels and syncs to recruiting systems.
4. **ATS workflow updates and recruiter copilot actions**
   - AI advances stages, drafts outreach, creates interview briefs, and logs structured notes.
5. **TA/HR self-service support with escalation rules**
   - Conversational AI handles repetitive policy/process questions and routes exceptions to humans.

## Best Use Case to Implement First
**Screening + coordination copilot (screening summaries + interview ops)**

### Why first
- Immediate cycle-time improvement with measurable throughput gains.
- Uses existing ATS/calendar data, so integration is usually tractable.
- Keeps human oversight in high-risk decisions while removing repetitive admin load.

## OpenClaw Workflow Mapping (v1)
1. **Trigger:** daily TA pipeline run (or event-triggered on new applicants)
2. **Ingest:** resumes, role scorecards, recruiter/hiring-manager criteria
3. **Classify:** fit tier, must-have match, risk/compliance flags, missing-data prompts
4. **Coordinate:** draft outreach + propose interview slots + send reminders
5. **Output:** recruiter action queue (advance / hold / reject / request manual review)

## Suggested Pilot KPIs (7–14 day pilot)
- time-to-screen (first review SLA)
- interview scheduling cycle time
- % pipeline records updated without manual rework
- shortlist acceptance rate by hiring managers
- recruiter hours saved per open req/week
