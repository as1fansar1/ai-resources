## Executive summary
The integration of AI into accounting workflows is rapidly evolving, with a focus on practical automation that enhances efficiency without sacrificing oversight. Businesses are increasingly adopting AI tools that streamline repetitive tasks, improve accuracy, and allow teams to focus on strategic decision-making. However, it is crucial to approach these technologies with a critical eye, ensuring that human oversight remains a key component in high-risk areas.

## Top 5 practical use cases
1. **Automated Invoice Processing**: Utilizing Optical Character Recognition (OCR) and machine learning to capture and categorize invoices, reducing manual entry errors and speeding up processing times.
2. **Accounts Payable (AP) Automation**: Implementing tools that automate the approval workflow for invoices, ensuring timely payments while maintaining compliance and audit trails.
3. **Accounts Receivable (AR) Automation**: Automating payment matching and dunning processes to enhance cash flow management and reduce the time spent on collections.
4. **Expense Management Automation**: Streamlining employee reimbursement processes through automated policy checks and approval routing, minimizing manual intervention.
5. **Financial Reporting Automation**: Generating recurring reports automatically, allowing teams to focus on analysis rather than data collection.

## Best first use case to implement
**Automated Invoice Processing** is the best first use case for businesses looking to pilot AI in accounting. This process is typically high-volume and repetitive, making it an ideal candidate for automation. By implementing an OCR solution, businesses can significantly reduce the time spent on data entry and improve accuracy. Human oversight should be maintained for exception handling and final approvals to mitigate risks associated with errors.

## OpenClaw workflow mapping
1. **Input Stage**: Capture invoices via email or upload.
2. **Processing Stage**: Use OCR to extract data and categorize invoices.
3. **Validation Stage**: Automatically match invoices against purchase orders.
4. **Approval Stage**: Route invoices for human approval if discrepancies are found.
5. **Output Stage**: Post approved invoices to the accounting system and notify relevant stakeholders.

## 7-day KPI pilot
- **Day 1**: Set up the OCR tool and integrate it with existing accounting software.
- **Day 2**: Train the system with historical invoice data to improve accuracy.
- **Day 3**: Begin processing a batch of invoices using the OCR tool.
- **Day 4**: Monitor the accuracy of data extraction and categorize errors.
- **Day 5**: Implement a review process for flagged invoices requiring human intervention.
- **Day 6**: Collect feedback from users on the workflow efficiency and accuracy.
- **Day 7**: Analyze KPIs such as processing time, error rates, and user satisfaction to evaluate the pilot's success.
