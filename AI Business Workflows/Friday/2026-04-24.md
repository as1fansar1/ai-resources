**Date pulled:** 2026-04-24 (Asia/Dhaka)

**Theme:** how businesses are using AI to automate/replace support and service operations  

## Executive summary
In the past month, businesses have increasingly adopted AI to streamline operations in customer support. The focus has shifted from merely automating tasks to enhancing workflows that improve efficiency and customer satisfaction. Practical implementations of AI, particularly through tools like OpenClaw, emphasize controlled automation with human oversight to mitigate risks. This report outlines actionable use cases, the best initial pilot project, and a framework for measuring success.

## Top 5 practical use cases
1. **Automated Ticket Routing and Classification**  
   AI systems can automatically categorize and route customer inquiries to the appropriate support teams, reducing response times and improving resolution rates.

2. **Self-Service Portals with AI Assistance**  
   Implementing AI-driven self-service options allows customers to resolve common issues independently, freeing up human agents for more complex queries.

3. **AI-Enhanced Knowledge Management**  
   AI can curate and suggest knowledge base articles based on customer queries, ensuring agents have quick access to relevant information and improving response accuracy.

4. **Proactive Customer Engagement**  
   AI tools can analyze customer behavior and trigger proactive outreach, such as reminders or follow-ups, enhancing customer experience and retention.

5. **Human-in-the-Loop Escalation Systems**  
   AI can handle routine inquiries but escalate complex issues to human agents, ensuring that customer interactions maintain a personal touch when necessary.

## Best first use case to implement
**Automated Ticket Routing and Classification** is the most practical first pilot project. This use case allows businesses to leverage existing ticketing systems and improve efficiency without requiring extensive changes to current workflows. By implementing AI to categorize and route tickets, organizations can achieve immediate benefits in response times and customer satisfaction.

## OpenClaw workflow mapping
1. **Input Stage**: Customer submits a query via email, chat, or web form.
2. **Processing Stage**: 
   - AI analyzes the query to determine intent and urgency.
   - Based on predefined rules, the system classifies the ticket and routes it to the appropriate department.
3. **Human Oversight**: 
   - Low-confidence classifications trigger alerts for human review.
   - Human agents can adjust classifications and provide feedback to improve AI accuracy.
4. **Resolution Stage**: 
   - AI suggests potential solutions based on historical data and knowledge base.
   - Human agents finalize responses and ensure customer satisfaction.

## 7-day KPI pilot
- **Day 1-2**: Implement AI routing in a controlled environment with a small volume of tickets.
- **Day 3-4**: Monitor ticket resolution times and customer satisfaction scores.
- **Day 5**: Gather feedback from agents on AI performance and areas for improvement.
- **Day 6**: Adjust AI parameters based on feedback and performance metrics.
- **Day 7**: Evaluate overall impact on operational efficiency and prepare a report for stakeholders.

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
- Average ticket resolution time
- Customer satisfaction score (CSAT)
- Percentage of tickets correctly classified by AI
- Agent workload reduction
